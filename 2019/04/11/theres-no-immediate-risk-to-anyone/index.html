There’s no immediate risk to anyone

You’ll need to follow 3 steps before confidentiality can be breached if there’s no immediate risk to anyone. You can’t decide whether to breach confidentiality yourself. You’ll need to:

  1. Speak to your chief officer
  2. Speak to the operations team (your chief officer might do this)
  3. Keep a written record of the situation and what’s been decided

Give your chief officer all the information you have about the situation. If they agree that confidentiality might need to be breached, they’ll contact the operations team or ask you to contact them.

If the situation is urgent, the operations team will aim to give you a decision in 1 hour. They’ll ask some questions about your situation, so make sure you gather all your evidence before you contact them.

Before making a decision about breaching confidentiality, the operations team will:

  • look at your evidence
  • think about how likely the risk is to the client
  • assess the impact of the risk

They’ll then tell you or your chief officer what the decision is. You’ll need to keep a record of the situation and the decision.
