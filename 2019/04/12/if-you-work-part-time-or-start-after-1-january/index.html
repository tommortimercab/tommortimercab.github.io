If you work part time or start after 1 January

If you work part time, your leave is usually calculated in hours. For example, if you work half time, you get 94 hours and 15 minutes of annual leave. This is the same as 13 days’ annual leave.

If you join part way through the year, you get a proportion of your leave in your first year.

You can use the leave calculator to work out how much leave you get.

You still get 4 Citizens Advice days if they fall on your usual work days.

If your working pattern changes

If you change your working pattern, the amount of annual leave you get will also change – for example if you go from full-time to part-time.

You can calculate how much leave you’ll get.

Taking bank holidays if you’re part time

You need to book off any bank holidays you’re due to work on. You’ll get extra hours added to your annual leave so you don’t miss out on time off for bank holidays. This is based on how many hours you work in a week.

If you don’t have enough annual leave left for a bank holiday, you can ask your line manager if you can work an extra day to make up for it. Check with them before you work an extra day.
