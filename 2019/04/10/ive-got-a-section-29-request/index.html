I’ve got a section 29 request

Don’t give out any information if you get a section 29 request.

You might get a ‘section 29’ request for information about a client from the police, a government department or a local authority – this includes benefit fraud investigators. They can request information that helps prevent and detect crimes, prosecute offenders and assess or collect tax.

Section 29 is part of the Data Protection Act. It means the organisation contacting you think they have a right to get information without having to get a client’s consent.

You’ll need to follow 3 steps if you get a section 29 request. You should:

  • check the Information Commissioner’s Office’s good practice guide for help
  • write to the organisation who sent the request
  • speak to the operations team about a possible breach of confidentiality if you think you’ve got relevant information
