Confidentiality policy and signing the declaration

Client confidentiality is a core part of the local Citizens Advice service, so it’s important you understand our policy. How it affects you will depend on your role in the Citizens Advice service.

All roles in a local Citizens Advice need to follow the confidentiality policy, including trustee local board members.

You’ll need to ensure that:

  • no information about a client goes outside the service without the client’s permission, unless there are exceptional circumstances
  • all interviews are held in private
  • you don’t discuss a client’s case where you can be overheard
  • clients aren’t asked to talk about the problem in front of others if they don’t want to, for example in the waiting room
  • referrals to another organisation are only made with the client’s consent
  • check how your client wants to be contacted and only contact them that way
  • make sure you don’t reveal confidential details when you contact them, for example in an email or letter

Confidentiality declaration

You’ll need to sign the declaration when you’ve read this confidentiality policy.

Signing the declaration means you understand the policy and agree to follow it.
