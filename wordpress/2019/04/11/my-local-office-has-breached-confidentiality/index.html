My local office has breached confidentiality

There are steps you’ll need to take if confidentiality has been breached. The steps you take depend on whether it was an accidental or deliberate breach of confidentiality.

If it’s an accidental breach of confidentiality

Some breaches of confidentiality could be accidental, for example if you’ve written to a client at the wrong address or sent information to the wrong client.

If you discover an unauthorised breach of confidentiality, you’ll need to:

  • speak to the operations team straight away, even if the breach is accidental
  • tell the client what’s happened and tell them they can make a formal complaint if they want to
  • speak to the members of staff or volunteers responsible for the branch
  • check your local office’s procedures are correct – if they’re not, update them
  • keep a record of the breach and any action you take

If it’s a deliberate breach of confidentiality

If a client’s confidentiality was breached deliberately, you’ll need to investigate the circumstances and take disciplinary action if necessary.

A serious and unauthorised breach of confidentiality could mean your local Citizens Advice is referred to the Membership and Standards Committee.

You’ll also need to:

  • report the incident to the operations team immediately
  • tell the client
